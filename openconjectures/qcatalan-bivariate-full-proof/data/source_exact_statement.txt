The one-variable q-Catalan definition gives C_(n,k)(q)=0 for k>n, and its
following remark uses that convention to saturate the k=n row. The later
q,p-Catalan definition separately displays
C_(n,k)(q,p)=C_(n,k-1)(q,p)+q^(n-k-1)p^k C_(n-1,k)(q,p) for 1<=k<=n, but does
not restate an out-of-range convention. Consequently its k=n line contains
an undefined C_(n-1,n)(q,p) term unless the natural saturation convention is
supplied.

Conjecture 5.4 states that, for every n and k, C_(n,k)(q,p) equals the
weighted inversion/co-inversion enumerator on the source's 312-avoiding
permutation class S'_(n,k).

This packet proves the stated bivariate interpretation for 0<=k<n and proves
the natural saturated completion at k=n. It discloses, rather than silently
repairs, the source's missing bivariate boundary convention.
